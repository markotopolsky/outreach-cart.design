# Lobo Gun Leathers — lead (EN, pain point odhalený)

US kožiar — holstery, 54 rokov v biznise. Tier 2 z Cart Leads CSV ([[cold-outreach-pipeline]]). Otvárač z [[outreach-batch-5]].

## Stav konverzácie

**Reagovala (k 17.7., zbadané v inboxe 20.7.):** objednávky idú výhradne cez telefón/email kvôli veľkému počtu variácií produktov, odhadovaný **16-týždňový** čas dodania. Silný signál operatívneho preťaženia — presne ICP (dopyt existuje, kanál je manuálny bottleneck). Marko odoslal 20.7. reveal + pitch na guided order form.

## Komunikácia (IG DM)

**Marko (otvárač, 16.7.):**
> Hey! 54 years making holsters is a serious run — that kind of longevity usually means a real waitlist. Curious how orders actually work day to day: mostly phone/email back and forth, or can people order straight off lobogunleather.com?

**Lobo Gun Leathers (k 17.7., prijaté v inboxe 20.7.):**
> We prefer to do the orders via phone and email just because there are so many different variations even with in the variations of the different models. We are guessing a 16 week turn around at this time too.

**Marko (20.7., cez Kimi WebBridge):**
> Makes sense with that many variations — phone/email probably beats a confusing dropdown menu. That said, 16 weeks is a long wait when it starts as an unstructured call. I build stores for makers, and a guided order form (walks someone through the exact variation options, captures everything up front before it hits your phone) could cut a lot of that back-and-forth without forcing anyone through a generic checkout. Worth a look, or does phone/email genuinely work fine for you as-is?
