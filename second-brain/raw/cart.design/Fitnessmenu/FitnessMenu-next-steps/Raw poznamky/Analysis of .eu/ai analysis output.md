## PHASE 1: AI-Origin Signals & Technical Risk

**1.1 The single biggest finding: the legal entity in the footer changes per route.** This is not a style nitpick; it's five different companies claiming to operate one storefront:

- `/` and `/kosik`: INVIZ s. r. o., Doležalova 3424/15C, Bratislava, IČO: 54374278
- `/objednat-balik` and `/dorucenie`: Real Estate Factory s. r. o., Hollého 740/3, 905 01 Senica, IČO: 54607027 — a company literally named "Real Estate Factory" selling meal boxes
- `/skusobny-balik`: INSUS s. r. o., Moyzesova 6065/4B, Pezinok, IČO: 53817311
- `/cennik`: Maboma Holding s. r. o. with a Bratislava address and yet another IČO
- `/jedalnicek`: Divitias Core s. r. o., IČO: 50942123

I re-fetched `/jedalnicek` and got the same entity both times, so it's stable per route (or cached), not random per request — which points at either a misconfigured multi-tenant/env variable baked into static builds, or footer data copy-pasted from cloned page templates and never reconciled. Either way: a customer comparing the order page to the trial page is buying from two different legal persons; a Stripe/ComGate merchant-of-record mismatch and a consumer-law problem, and the loudest "nobody read the output" signal on the whole site.

**1.2 Broken slug generation on `/dorucenie`.** The city list contains systematically corrupted URL slugs where Slovak diacritics were mis-transliterated: Dežerice → /dorucenie/deyerice, Podlužany → /dorucenie/podluyany, Žabokreky nad Nitrou → /dorucenie/uabokreky-nad-nitrou, plus koreca (Košeca), bolerov (Bolešov), hrarne (Hrašné), ltka (Lúka), nemrova (Nemšová), dolna-stca (Dolná Súča), and more. The mapping is consistent (ž→y/u, š→r, ú→t), meaning a hand-rolled slugify with a wrong character table ran across the whole dataset and shipped. Correct slugs coexist on the same page (e.g. `sastin-straze` handles š/ž fine), so two different normalizers exist in the codebase. This is a concrete tell of generated code without review: the bug is invisible unless someone actually clicks the links, and it torpedoes the local-SEO strategy those 238 city pages exist for.

**1.3 Contradictory business facts across pages — copy generated per-page, no single source of truth:**

- Delivery days: the homepage delivery card says "Jedlo na Nedeľa – Štvrtok · doručíme deň vopred" while the identical card on `/dorucenie` says "Jedlo na Pondelok – Piatok · doručíme deň vopred". The homepage version conflated _delivery_ days (Sun–Thu) with _food_ days (Mon–Fri) — same component, different hardcoded strings.
- Delivery price: the homepage FAQ says home delivery costs 4,60 € and pickup points 3 €; `/dorucenie` labels the same home delivery "Zadarmo" (free); `/cennik` says "Podľa mesta" (depends on the city). Three answers to "what does delivery cost" depending on which page you land on. For your target user this is exactly the "something feels off" moment that kills a checkout.
- City count, same page: the `/dorucenie` coverage card says 242 miest while the body text three sections later says 238 miest a obcí in 4 kraje — and the per-region counts (41+89+99+9) sum to 238. The 242 is stale data in a shared card.
- Program naming: the homepage FAQ describes five named programs — Office, Racio, Veget, Muscle, Low Carb — but the configurator sells four generic types (Vyvážené / Bez mäsa / Viac bielkovín / Low-carb) with no "Racio" anywhere. A testimonial also references "Program Muscle." Legacy brand architecture survives in FAQ copy and reviews while the purchase flow speaks a different vocabulary; a shopper reading FAQ → configurator can't map one onto the other.

**1.4 Unpolished details actually visible in markup:**

- `/kosik` has a doubled title tag: "Košík | Fitnessmenu.sk | Fitnessmenu.sk" — a title template applied on top of an already-suffixed string.
- The trial page's sample menu day is hardcoded as "Piatok 1.5." — May 1st, viewed in July, and a Slovak state holiday on which their own blackout logic wouldn't deliver. Static demo data left in production.
- Same sample menu: "Quacamole s macesom" — misspelled Guacamole on the page whose entire job is proving food quality.
- On `/jedalnicek` the day view renders meals in the order Desiata → Obed → Olovrant → Večera → **Raňajky last** — breakfast sorted to the end of the day in the DOM. Looks like an unordered map render or a wrong sort key.
- In the rendered header, the contact and cart links carry the literal path strings "/kontakt" and "/kosik" as their visible text next to "Objednať" and "Účet". This may be an icon-button fallback/aria artifact — I can't confirm without inspecting the hydrated DOM — but as server-rendered text content it's raw paths in the nav.
- The homepage testimonial section server-renders the same reviews (Peter Benčo, Tereza T., Jana Hochel…) dozens of times over — a marquee/carousel duplicated in markup rather than cloned in JS. Cosmetically invisible, but it bloats the document and reads as a component nobody inspected.

**1.5 Frontend symptoms → backend risk inference (inference, not diagnosis).** The configurator's price renders as "— €/ deň" and "10 dní = —" in SSR, and the trial calculator ships with "Cena sa načítava…" — all pricing is resolved client-side after hydration. If that pattern reflects the backend generally, the risks are: (a) a pricing-API failure leaves a fully interactive configurator with a dead Continue button and no price, with no visible error-state copy anywhere in the markup to suggest one was designed; (b) price is presumably computed client-side or fetched then trusted — whether the server re-validates price at order creation is unverifiable from here, but the five-entities footer and two slugify implementations suggest a codebase where "the same logic in two places" is the norm, and price logic duplicated between the configurator, trial widget, and `/cennik` table is exactly where silent drift happens (the three contradictory delivery prices are arguably that drift already, in copy form). (c) The trial-abuse check is described in customer-facing copy in developer voice — "Pri checkoute server overí, či pre tento e-mail alebo telefón už neexistuje objednávka, ak áno, transakcia bude odmietnutá" — which is both a tell (spec language leaked into marketing) and a hint the dedupe key is just email+phone equality, trivially bypassed and prone to false-positives on shared households. I could not observe error handling, duplicate-submission behavior, or idempotency anywhere, so I make no claim about them beyond: nothing visible suggests they were designed.

**What's competently done (Phase 1 credit where due):** proper Next.js SSR with real content in the HTML (good for SEO and first paint), correct canonical/OG/meta hygiene on most routes, a coherent design token (`theme-color #f33f7f`) used consistently, Cloudflare email obfuscation, a GeoNames attribution line (someone cared about data licensing), and the 238 city landing pages are a genuinely sound programmatic-SEO play — undermined only by the slug bug.

## PHASE 2: Workflow & Cognitive Load

**2.1 Discovery is actually one of the site's strengths — with one structural flaw.** The configurator collapses the classic "which program?" paralysis into four sequential questions: meals per day (Celý deň 5 chodov / Do práce 3 chody), diet type (4 options), kcal tier (5 labeled levels from "Výrazné chudnutie" to "Silový šport"), duration, plus a "Chcete poradiť?" advisor that picks the energy level for you. Plain-language kcal labels tied to goals is exactly right for a non-technical buyer. The flaw: the configurator lives twice (homepage and `/objednat-balik`, apparently the same embedded component), and neither instance shows a single dish. The "what will I actually eat" evidence lives on `/jedalnicek`, a separate top-nav destination, and the menu page in turn has no configuration state — so the user ping-pongs between "configure blind" and "browse food with no way to act on it." For a time-starved buyer, that's the tab-switching loop that ends in abandonment. The `/jedalnicek` cards themselves show name + allergens but, in the server-rendered output, no kcal/macros per dish despite the page promising "plnou nutričnou hodnotou" — if macros only appear on click/hydration, the page under-delivers its own headline; unverified whether hydration adds them.

**2.2 Allergy/exclusion handling is deferred out of the purchase flow entirely.** There is no allergen step in the 4-step configurator. Per the FAQ, you mark excluded allergens (the 14 EU-mandatory ones) at registration, and the system auto-swaps unsafe dishes; if no alternative exists that day, they contact you before delivery. The auto-substitution engine is a genuinely strong mechanic — better than competitors that just print warnings. But sequencing it after purchase means a lactose-intolerant buyer configures, pays, and only then learns whether the service can feed her. That's backwards for trust: the allergen question is a _pre-purchase anxiety_, and the current flow answers it post-payment. There's also no per-dish exclusion ("I hate fish") anywhere visible — the swap policy is max 2 meal swaps, only within the 10-day package, only in the account, 48h before delivery, which is restrictive enough that picky eaters (a large share of this demographic) have no lever during purchase.

**Unverified:** the actual interaction quality of the slider ("Potiahni posuvník pre vlastný počet dní" 2–30 days), touch targets, and all mobile breakpoints — I could not render viewports. Flagging rather than guessing.

## PHASE 3: Revenue Leak & Business Logic

**3.1 Trial mechanics — mapped as built.** The "trial" is a _paid_ 2-day, 10-meal package: price according to the configured package (so roughly 2 × 19,40–25,40 € ≈ 39–51 € at `/cennik` rates), with free delivery, one per customer, gated 1× per email + phone with a server-side rejection, and a 15 € discount on the next package granted after trying. Verdict: **this is financially sound** — it's not a loss-leader free trial; the company's exposure per trial is the delivery subsidy (their own FAQ prices it at 4,60 €) plus the contingent 15 € coupon that only costs money on _conversion_. Food is paid at near-full margin. Where money actually leaks:

- **The hero price is either wrong or unexplainable.** The homepage advertises "od 11,90 € / deň", but the visible `/cennik` matrix bottoms out at 18,40 €/deň (subscription, lightest tier, 5 courses). The 11,90 plausibly belongs to the 3-course "Do práce" tab I couldn't expand — but if the flagship number requires switching to a tab the pricing page doesn't open by default, every full-day buyer experiences a ~70% price jump between hero and checkout. That's the classic anchor-then-disappoint leak, and it burns exactly the trust the trial exists to build. Unverified which tier 11,90 maps to; the leak is the _gap_, which is verified.
- **Trial ≠ subscription bridge is a coupon, not a flow.** The 15 € discount is mentioned in copy, but nothing visible converts trial state into a pre-filled subscription (same config, one click). Post-trial monetization apparently depends on the customer returning and re-configuring from scratch.
- The dedupe key (email+phone) is weak against abuse and, worse, hostile to legitimate cases — two people in one household sharing a phone number get one trial between them, per the stated rule.

**3.2 Hook vs. flow.** The time-saving hook is executed well above template grade — the homepage quantifies it (3h shopping, 9h cooking, 4h cleaning per week; 50 days of life back per year), which is distinctive, on-persona copy, not boilerplate. The freshness hook ("Každé ráno varíme z čerstvých surovín. Žiadne mrazené polotovary.") is asserted but never evidenced inside the flow — no cook-date on the menu cards, no "cooked tomorrow morning for your Wednesday box" line in the configurator summary. And the flow actively contradicts the "zero friction" hook at its most important moment: the configurator summary panel shows "— €/deň" until JS resolves, and delivery scheduling is explicitly punted — "Dátum prvého doručenia... si nastavíš v ďalšom kroku (košík)" — so the user commits to a package before knowing whether Thursday delivery to her PSČ even works. The PSČ availability check exists, but on `/dorucenie`, disconnected from purchase.

**3.3 Retention & upsell gaps — and one thing done right.** Done right: proteins as a parallel product line with cross-country courier logistics, and the cart explicitly supports mixed orders ("Krabičky aj proteíny môžete mať v jednej objednávke"); skip-a-day converts to 12-month credit with no fee, which is a solid churn-prevention primitive; gift vouchers and B2B bulk vouchers with single invoice exist. Missing entirely, based on everything visible: no protein cross-sell _inside_ the configurator (the products live in nav and footer, not in the flow where a "add COMPLEX to your box, delivered together, save courier fee" line would print money); no visible pause/downgrade path marketed for the subscription (only skip-day); no post-trial email/flow evidence (unverifiable, but nothing on-site references it beyond the coupon); the 2-swap limit exists only on 10-day packages, so subscription customers — the highest-LTV segment — apparently have _less_ menu flexibility than a one-off 10-day buyer, which is inverted.

## PHASE 4: Checkout & Backend Friction

Honest limit first: `/kosik` server-renders only a header and the sentence "Skontrolujte si položky a pokračujte na pokladňu" — the entire cart/checkout UI is client-side, so I could not enumerate form fields, count steps, or see the delivery date-picker. What the copy does establish:

- **Account pressure exists but forced registration is unconfirmed.** Every page ships a login modal (SMS, Google, Apple, email+password — a genuinely modern auth mix), and the allergen system is tied to registration per FAQ. Whether guest checkout exists is unverifiable; if allergen exclusion requires an account, then any allergic customer is de facto forced to register mid-checkout.
- **No express wallet is mentioned anywhere.** Payment methods per FAQ: card via Stripe, meal vouchers (Edenred, Up, Doxx, Ticket Restaurant) via ComGate, or bank transfer. The meal-voucher integration is a smart, locally decisive feature — genuinely a competitive strength for the office demographic. But Apple Pay/Google Pay are never named; Stripe _can_ provide them, and whether it's enabled is unverifiable. Given the persona, if wallets aren't on, that's the single cheapest conversion fix available. Bank transfer as an option for perishable-food logistics is also an ops risk in itself (order confirmed, kitchen capacity reserved, payment arrives in 2 days or never).
- **Scheduling model:** first-delivery date, delivery days, and skip days are all configured in cart, with a 48-hour cutoff that varies by city, shown automatically at order time, and blackout holidays that auto-shift delivery with an SMS 3 days ahead. The blackout-with-SMS design is competent. The date-picker UI itself: cannot assess, not rendered server-side.
