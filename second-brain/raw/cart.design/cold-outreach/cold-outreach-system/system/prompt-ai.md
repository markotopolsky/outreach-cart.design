**Task:**  

Create **2 variants of a cold outreach message** for a freelancer / agency specializing in e-commerce shop development. Each message must follow **specific rules and structure** outlined below.  


---

  

### Rules for the Message:

**Message Structure:**  

1. **OPEN** — Start with a **specific observation** from the client’s online presence (e.g., an unexpected detail on their shop, a technical issue, a missing feature). **Never start with yourself** (e.g., “Good day, I’m calling because…” / “I’m reaching out because…”).  

2. **IDENTIFY THE PROBLEM/OPPORTUNITY** — Be specific about what the client is missing or what could be improved. **Avoid vague statements** (e.g., “performance is weak” → “pages load in 5 seconds on mobile”).  

3. **PROPOSE A SOLUTION** — Briefly, 1-2 sentences. What would be the impact (e.g., “If we optimized speed, you could gain 20% more customers”).  

4. **CLOSE WITH A SOFT CTA** — e.g., “Would it be worth discussing this further?” / “Could we schedule a 15-minute call?”  

  

**Tone and Style:**  

- Adjust the **tone** based on the `tone` field (professional / friendly / direct).  

- Use the **language** specified in the `language` field (SK / CZ / EN).  

- Be **authentic**, not salesy. Write like a human, not a template.  

- **Avoid:**  

  - Clichés (e.g., “in the digital age,” “today’s world”).  

  - Generic phrases (e.g., “your shop is unique”).  

  - Superlatives about yourself (e.g., “the best,” “top team”).  

  

**Message Length:**  

- **Email:** 80-150 words, may include a subject line.  

- **LinkedIn:** 50-100 words, conversational tone.  

- **Instagram/Facebook DM:** 30-70 words, informal and concise.  

- **WhatsApp/SMS:** 30-50 words, very concise and direct.
